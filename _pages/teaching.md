      <h3>Engineering Management Capstone (ENGM90016)</h3>
      <p>
        ENGM90016 is a graduate coursework capstone subject that asks students to work in teams and integrate engineering
        and project management principles, contract and procurement strategies, finance, and management strategy into
        business-case decision making. My teaching support is positioned around helping students move from ambiguous
        project contexts to structured proposals, defensible budgets, risk-aware plans, and senior-level communication.
      </p>
      <div class="teaching-impact">
        <strong>Teaching outcome</strong>
        <span>ENGM90016 achieved Top 20% of ESS Scores in EMI in Semester 2 of 2025.</span>
      </div>
      <ul class="teaching-list">
        <li>Supported team-based project formulation, business-case logic, and proposal presentation.</li>
        <li>Emphasized decision criteria, economic and financial reasoning, risk management, and project control.</li>
        <li>Connected engineering management practice with communication, leadership, and collaborative problem solving.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>AI Enabled Architectural Programming and Design</h3>
      <p>
        This graduate design course uses child-friendly urban public space as the programming and design context, bringing
        together architectural programming theory, AI technology, and studio-based design practice. The course links social
        investigation, public needs analysis, AI-assisted brief generation, design iteration, and multi-stakeholder
        evaluation into one workflow.
      </p>
      <ul class="teaching-list teaching-list--columns">
        <li>Guided students in framing child-friendly public-space problems through social context and field research.</li>
        <li>Supported the use of architectural programming methods to collect, clean, and structure design evidence.</li>
        <li>Connected LLM and AIGC workflows with task-book development, concept generation, and design assessment.</li>
        <li>Helped students prepare staged deliverables from site research to final design review.</li>
